# The game is balanced trade-first, with deliberately defender-favored combat

The primary loop is economic: Teams interact mainly through Trade (via the Marketplace), and War is a secondary pressure valve, not the centerpiece. To keep it that way, combat is deliberately tilted against aggressors:

- Defending is easier and less risky than attacking.
- A single raid never captures a Tile; conquest requires sustained pressure (the graduated Resources → Buildings → Units → Tile loss model).
- A failed attack *rewards the defender* — attacker Units are lost or captured, the attacker loses Money — so attacking is genuinely high-risk/high-reward.
- New Teams get time-based Beginner's protection, and no Team is ever permanently destroyed (Respawn).

We chose this over a war-centric or attacker-favored balance because the game's identity is a grounded, social, economy-first world; cheap, profitable aggression would collapse it into a raiding game and drive out the builders and traders it is designed around. The cost is that players seeking constant PvP may find war too punishing — an accepted trade-off.

## Consequences

- Combat math, raid yields, and capture thresholds must be tuned so attacking is rarely worth it unless the attacker heavily out-powers the defender.
- War becomes meaningful mainly for contesting scarce/occupied Tiles, not for routine income.
