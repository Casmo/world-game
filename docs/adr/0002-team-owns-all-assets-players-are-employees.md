# Teams own all assets; players are employees, not landowners

A Team (player-facing: "Clan") collectively owns every Tile, City, Building, and Resource. Individual players never own land or cities. Players contribute labor (constructing and working Buildings) and receive individual payment from the Team; their progression is rank/skill/wealth *within* the company.

We chose this "company / employee" model over players owning their own tiles (with the Team as a loose aggregate of personal holdings) because it makes the Team the meaningful unit of play: a shared treasury worth defending, a real internal economy, and wars that target a collective rather than one person's plot. It maps cleanly onto the existing starter-kit `Team` model's roles and permissions (who may spend the treasury, set wages, or declare war).

## Consequences

- Individual motivation cannot rely on land ownership; a separate personal-progression system (rank, skills, titles, wealth) is required to keep solo play rewarding.
- Wage/payment allocation becomes a social and political mechanic internal to each Team.
